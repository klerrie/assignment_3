PURCHASE APPROVAL PROCESS

How to get approval for purchases and procurement.

APPROVAL THRESHOLDS
- Under $500: Manager approval
- $500-$2,500: Department head approval
- $2,500-$10,000: VP approval
- $10,000-$50,000: CFO approval
- Over $50,000: Board approval required
- Capital: Different thresholds for capital items

PURCHASE REQUESTS
- System: Submit through procurement portal
- Details: Item, quantity, vendor, cost
- Justification: Business case required
- Budget: Verify budget availability
- Timeline: Expected delivery date
- Approval: Route to appropriate approver

PURCHASE ORDERS
- Creation: Auto-created after approval
- Number: Unique PO number assigned
- Terms: Payment terms specified
- Vendor: Sent to vendor
- Tracking: Track PO status in system
- Receiving: Match to receipt

PROCUREMENT PROCESS
- Vendor selection: Use preferred vendors
- Quotes: Get 3 quotes for large purchases
- Contracts: Legal review for contracts
- Negotiation: Procurement negotiates terms
- Approval: Follow approval workflow
- Purchase: Issue purchase order

CAPITAL EXPENDITURES
- Definition: Equipment, software, assets
- Approval: Higher approval thresholds
- Depreciation: Tracked as assets
- Budget: Separate capital budget
- Justification: ROI analysis required
- Board: Board approval for large items

SOFTWARE AND SUBSCRIPTIONS
- IT review: IT must approve software
- Security: Security review required
- Licensing: Verify licensing compliance
- Budget: Annual subscription costs
- Renewal: Track renewal dates
- Centralized: IT manages software portfolio

TRAVEL AND EVENTS
- Travel: Follow travel policy
- Events: Conference and event approval
- Budget: Travel budget allocation
- Advance: Can request travel advance
- Approval: Manager and finance approval
- Documentation: Keep all receipts

URGENT PURCHASES
- Process: Expedited approval available
- Justification: Strong business case needed
- Approval: Can get verbal approval, confirm in writing
- Documentation: Submit request after purchase
- Limits: Still subject to approval limits
- Review: Post-purchase review required

VENDOR MANAGEMENT
- Preferred: Use preferred vendor list
- New vendors: Vendor setup required
- Contracts: Legal review for contracts
- Terms: Negotiate favorable terms
- Performance: Track vendor performance
- Relationships: Maintain vendor relationships
