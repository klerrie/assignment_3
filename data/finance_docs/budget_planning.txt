BUDGET PLANNING AND MANAGEMENT

How budgets are created, managed, and monitored.

BUDGET CYCLE
- Planning: Annual budget planning in Q4
- Timeline: Budget due by December 1
- Review: Finance reviews and consolidates
- Approval: Executive team approves in January
- Effective: Budget year runs January to December
- Updates: Quarterly budget reviews

BUDGET COMPONENTS
- Personnel: Salaries, benefits, contractors
- Operating expenses: Supplies, software, services
- Capital expenditures: Equipment, infrastructure
- Travel: Business travel and events
- Marketing: Advertising, campaigns, events
- Professional services: Consultants, legal, accounting

BUDGET PREPARATION
- Template: Standard budget template provided
- Historical: Review previous year actuals
- Forecast: Project next year needs
- Justification: Required for increases
- Documentation: Supporting details required
- Submission: Through budget portal

BUDGET APPROVAL
- Department: Department head reviews
- Finance: Finance team consolidates
- Executive: C-suite final approval
- Board: Board approval for total budget
- Timeline: 4-6 week approval process
- Communication: Approved budgets communicated

BUDGET MONITORING
- Reports: Monthly budget vs actual reports
- Variance: Review significant variances
- Forecast: Quarterly reforecast
- Alerts: Automated alerts for overruns
- Meetings: Monthly budget review meetings
- Actions: Corrective actions for variances

BUDGET ADJUSTMENTS
- Transfers: Move between line items
- Increases: Require approval and justification
- Decreases: Can reduce if not needed
- Process: Submit budget adjustment request
- Approval: Based on amount and type
- Timing: Quarterly adjustment windows

CAPITAL BUDGETS
- Planning: Separate capital budget process
- Approval: Higher approval thresholds
- Depreciation: Tracked separately
- Assets: Tagged and tracked in system
- Disposal: Process for asset disposal
- Reporting: Capital budget reports monthly

VARIANCE ANALYSIS
- Threshold: Variances over 10% investigated
- Causes: Identify root causes
- Trends: Analyze trends over time
- Actions: Develop corrective actions
- Documentation: Document findings
- Follow-up: Track action completion
